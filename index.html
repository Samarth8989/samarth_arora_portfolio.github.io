Kirana Club

Driving GTV growth by unlocking orders across 2.5M+ retailers through strategic product interventions and data-driven optimization

PProblem Statement

Monthly GTV wasn't growing in proportion to the platform's retailer base. While many retailers were active, they weren't placing orders consistently, creating a disconnect between user acquisition and revenue growth.

"Despite having a strong retailer base, order frequency remained low, indicating deeper friction points in the user journey that needed systematic investigation."

RResearch & Insights

After analyzing app usage data, machine-level logs, and user feedback, the research revealed three critical friction points:

1Logistics Expansion & Prioritization

Observed a direct correlation between delivery delays and order cancellations. Retailers facing late deliveries took significantly longer to reorder.

  • Introduced ₹50 non-refundable confirmation fee for orders above ₹5,000
  • Onboarded new 3PL partners to expand serviceable pin codes
  • Prioritized faster fulfillment for higher-value orders

2Loyalty Program Architecture

Launched a structured loyalty program tied to repeat behavior to drive consistent ordering patterns.

  • Tier slabs based on cumulative monthly order value
  • Progressive rewards for higher engagement levels
  • Incentives designed for retailers across all purchase ranges

3Journey-Based Engagement

Retailers completed high-intent actions but often dropped off without placing orders. Implemented targeted nudge system.

  • Triggered by tier upgrades, reward unlocks, and product views
  • Multi-channel delivery: push, in-app, and WhatsApp
  • Personalized by region, order pattern, and user tier

Key Learnings

  • Small financial commitments (₹50 fee) significantly reduced cancellation behavior
  • Loyalty programs work best when structured around existing user behavior patterns
  • Multi-channel engagement increases effectiveness of retention campaigns
  • Data-driven expansion of logistics creates compound growth effects

Azgo

Driving order volume growth through optimized user experience and data-driven inventory management

PProblem Statement

Despite Azgo's machines being strategically placed in high-density office spaces, order volume growth remained slower than expected. While footfall was high, users also dropped off after opening the app to place orders.

IResearch & Insights

After analyzing app usage data, machine-level logs, and user feedback, the analysis revealed three key frictions:

1High Drop-offs

High drop-offs during snack selection or payment, especially among first-time users

2Inventory Mismatch

Inventory didn't match user preferences: top SKUs went out of stock quickly, and there was growing demand for healthier options

3Low Order Frequency

Users placed one order and didn't return due to low novelty or incentive

SSolution Strategy

1Redesign of ordering flow to boost conversion

  • Redesigned the ordering flow to reduce clicks and load time
  • Introduced one-tap combos and limited-time discounts
  • Added micro-rewards like scratch cards to gamify purchases and increase completion rates

2Smarter Inventory Planning

  • Built machine-level dashboards to track SKU movement by location and time of day
  • Enabled low-stock alerts for refill prioritization
  • Optimized product mix using machine-level data, giving more slots to high order frequency SKUs and adding high-value healthy snacks

3Retention Loops to Drive Repeat Usage

  • Launched wallet top-ups with small cashback incentives to encourage reorders
  • Triggered push + WhatsApp nudges when new items were stocked or favorites returned

Key Learnings

  • Machine-level data analytics are crucial for optimizing inventory mix and reducing stockouts
  • Gamification elements like scratch cards significantly improve conversion rates
  • Location-specific inventory optimization leads to better user satisfaction
  • Multi-channel engagement drives repeat usage effectively

FlipTalk

Making real-time voice interaction in games both safe and playful through anonymous voice filters

GThe Genesis

FlipTalk was born out of firsthand experience. As a college gamer, I saw how quickly voice chats with strangers could turn abusive, especially when the team started losing or things didn't go as planned. Many peers around me faced the same issue: while multiplayer games were fun, the experience often turned unpleasant under pressure.

Curious to explore the space further, I came across Voicemod, a platform with 2M+ users working on real-time audio effects. After speaking with members of their team and understanding their use cases, I saw a clear opportunity to build something different, a platform that made real-time voice interaction in games both safe and playful.

PProduct Strategy

1Voice-Filtered Gaming for Anonymous Fun

  • Integrated real-time voice filters, allowing users to stay anonymous while still expressing emotion
  • Added playful personas like chipmunk, robot, and horror to make sessions fun and less intimidating

2User-Centric Feedback Loop

  • Launched an in-app feedback system to gather real-time insights on discomfort, fun level, and technical issues
  • Prioritized iterative improvements — voice lag, UI friction, filter control — based on feedback
  • This loop led to a 13% reduction in churn within 3 months

3Community-Led Growth & Retention

  • Built a 100-member campus ambassador network to drive low-cost user acquisition across colleges
  • Launched meme + influencer campaigns to make FlipTalk viral within student communities
  • Introduced loyalty-based XP rewards and "streak bonuses" to increase engagement and session repeat rate

4Recognition & Acceleration Support

  • FlipTalk was incubated at 3 accelerators — IIIT-H, AIC-SMUTBI, and Wadhwani Foundation
  • Received funding and awards from 2 of them, including Meta XR, which recognized FlipTalk among India's top 20 XR startups

Key Learnings

  • Anonymous voice filters significantly reduce toxicity in gaming environments
  • Campus ambassador networks are highly effective for student-focused products
  • Real-time feedback loops are crucial for iterative product improvement
  • Gamification through XP and streaks drives long-term engagement

Uber: Solving Post-Booking Anxiety

Addressing uncertainty and building trust in the critical moments after ride confirmation

PProblem

After the ride is confirmed, users experience a short-lived sense of relief, followed by uncertainty. Questions like "Why aren't they moving?", "Should I book again on another app?" or "Will the driver actually show up?" creates anxiety.

This is especially critical for time-sensitive use cases like airport drops, early morning rides, or meetings — where trust is as important as speed.

IInsight

From my own usage and conversations with friends who are also Uber users, I noticed a consistent pattern: people tend to check the map obsessively right after booking. If the car doesn't move within a minutes, they either call the driver, open a competing app, or cancel the ride.

This behavior isn't driven by ETA alone, it comes from a lack of certainty during a high-trust moment.

SWhat I would do as a PM at Uber

1Contextual communication

Replace ETAs with reason-based ETA updates.

Examples: "Driver is waiting at a traffic light before turning toward pickup"

This reduces the anxiety caused by a stationary car icon.

2Driver Monitoring & Parallel reassignment

If the assigned driver doesn't begin moving within a set threshold (e.g. 2 minutes), the system first nudges the driver to start the trip:

"Your rider is waiting. Please begin the journey to avoid cancellation."

If the driver still remains idle, Uber quietly begins searching for a backup driver in parallel, without notifying the user and creating unnecessary panic.

3Ride Lock Feature

Allow users to "lock" a driver for ₹50 at the time of booking. If the driver cancels within the next 2–3 minutes, the user receives a ₹100 credit.

This monetizes reliability for time-critical rides — like airport or business trips — and gives users peace of mind early in the booking journey.

MMetrics to Track

Post-Match Cancellation Rate

A direct measure of user anxiety after a driver is confirmed. Should drop as transparency and control improve.

Driver Call Frequency

Indicates trust gaps. May remain steady initially, but should decrease over time as users become familiar with clearer, in-app updates.

Ride Lock Adoption Rate

Reflects user demand for reliability during high-stakes situations.

Support Ticket Volume

Lower inbound support = better communication, less confusion, and smoother UX.

LinkedIn: Intelligent Feed Curation

Transforming LinkedIn's feed through smart content tagging and personalized professional interest matching

CThe Challenge

LinkedIn's feed often feels cluttered with irrelevant content. Users see posts about industries they're not interested in, career levels that don't match their stage, or topics unrelated to their professional goals, leading to decreased engagement and platform value.

SSolution Framework

1Smart Content Tagging

Automatically categorize posts by industry, role level, skill area, and content type using ML-powered content analysis.

2Professional Interest Profiles

Allow users to set preferences for industries, roles, company sizes, and content types they want to see more or less of.

3Role-Relevant Filtering

Surface content appropriate to user's career stage - leadership content for managers, skill-building for early career, industry insights for specialists.

4Feedback Loop

Implement quick reaction options (More like this / Less like this) to continuously improve recommendation accuracy.

BBusiness Impact

More relevant feeds would increase daily active usage, time spent on platform, and engagement rates. This directly supports LinkedIn's business model by creating more valuable ad inventory and improving recruiter tools effectiveness through better user profiling.

"Personalized professional content discovery drives deeper platform engagement and creates more valuable user profiles for LinkedIn's business ecosystem."
