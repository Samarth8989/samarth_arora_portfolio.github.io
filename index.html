What I Built

Retailer Display Contest

Launched monthly competitions where retailers could earn ₹500-2000 for creating attractive brand displays, with a guaranteed ₹100 participation reward. Implemented photo verification system and created scoring criteria based on display creativity, product placement, and customer engagement. This gamified approach motivated retailers to actively promote small brands instead of just stocking them.

Impact: 40% increase in retailer participation within first month

Brand Activation Intelligence

Developed a comprehensive scoring system that tracked retailer performance through photo verification and sales data. Created personalized dashboards showing each retailer the ROI of promoting specific brands, including metrics like inventory turnover rate, customer repeat purchases, and profit margins. This data-driven approach helped retailers understand which small brands were worth actively promoting.

Impact: 22% AOV increase as retailers consolidated orders for higher rewards

Margin Arbitrage Engine

Created a subscription model where small brands paid ₹50/month per participating store for guaranteed visibility through our display program. Used the performance data from displays to demonstrate value to brands - showing increased sales, brand awareness, and market penetration. This created a sustainable revenue stream while solving the brand pull problem at the retailer level.

The Aha Moment

"We had machines selling ₹3K/month while others hit ₹25K—same building, different floors. Traditional snacks offered 12-15% margins with constant price pressure from distributors.

During routine site visits, I started noticing patterns: machines near meeting areas sold more afternoon snacks, but office workers kept asking 'do you have protein bars?' We were stocking what vendors pushed, not what office workers actually wanted."

What I Built

Strategic Machine Repositioning

Relocated machines within offices to better locations after observing actual usage patterns and negotiating with facility managers

Wellness Snacking Portfolio

Partnered with healthy snack brands offering 35% margins and positioned select machines as 'office wellness corners'

Demand-Based Inventory Strategy

Piloted wellness-focused inventory on 120 machines: 27% healthy options during peak hours, energy items for late workers

Solving Post-Booking Anxiety

Addressing uncertainty and building trust in the critical moments after ride confirmation

Problem

After the ride is confirmed, users experience a short-lived sense of relief, followed by uncertainty. Questions like "Why aren't they moving?", "Should I book again on another app?" or "Will the driver actually show up?" creates anxiety.

This is especially critical for time-sensitive use cases like airport drops, early morning rides, or meetings — where trust is as important as speed.

Insight

From my own usage and conversations with friends who are also Uber users, I noticed a consistent pattern: people tend to check the map obsessively right after booking. If the car doesn't move within a minutes, they either call the driver, open a competing app, or cancel the ride.

This behavior isn't driven by ETA alone, it comes from a lack of certainty during a high-trust moment.

What I would do as a PM at Uber

Contextual Communication

Replace ETAs with reason-based ETA updates. Examples: "Driver is waiting at a traffic light before turning toward pickup". This reduces the anxiety caused by a stationary car icon.

Driver Monitoring & Parallel Reassignment

If the assigned driver doesn't begin moving within a set threshold (e.g. 2 minutes), the system first nudges the driver to start the trip. If the driver still remains idle, Uber quietly begins searching for a backup driver in parallel, without creating unnecessary panic.

Nudge to Rebook When Driver Doesn't Move

Allow users to "lock" a driver for ₹50 at the time of booking. If the driver cancels within the next 2–3 minutes, the user receives a ₹100 credit. This monetizes reliability for time-critical rides.

Impact

These changes aim to solve not just logistical issues, but emotional friction. By addressing post-booking anxiety through transparency, backup systems, and reliability monetization, we can build deeper trust during critical moments.

Metrics to Track

Post-Match Cancellation Rate

A direct measure of user anxiety after a driver is confirmed. Should drop as transparency and control improve.

Driver Call Frequency

Indicates trust gaps. May remain steady initially, but should decrease over time as users become familiar with clearer, in-app updates.

Ride Lock Adoption Rate

Reflects user demand for reliability during high-stakes situations.

Support Ticket Volume

Lower inbound support = better communication, less confusion, and smoother UX.

Building LinkedIn's Feed Relevance

Transforming the professional feed from clickbait-driven to career-focused through intelligent content curation and user preferences

Problem

LinkedIn is meant to help professionals discover content that supports career growth, from job opportunities and upskilling to industry insights.

However, the feed is filled with clickbaity posts that often feel generic or attention-seeking. People find one post going viral and many others start posting that same content, this makes it spammy. Users scroll through content that doesn't match their interests, role, or goals, making the platform feel less useful over time.

What I would do as a PM at LinkedIn

Tag-Based Content Preferences

Allow users to select specific topics, skills, and industry areas they want to see more of. Use these preferences to weight content in the algorithm and surface more relevant posts.

Role-Specific Content Surfacing

Leverage job title and industry data to surface content that's specifically relevant to the user's current role and career stage. Product managers see product strategy content, designers see design thinking posts.

Anti-Viral Content Detection

Implement systems to detect when similar content templates are being overused. Reduce distribution of copycat posts and reward original, thoughtful content.

Content Quality Scoring

Develop quality metrics based on engagement depth (comments vs. likes), save rates, and time spent reading. Prioritize substantive content over viral but shallow posts.

Expected Impact

By focusing on relevance over virality, LinkedIn can become a more valuable professional tool. Users will spend more meaningful time on the platform, engage with content that actually helps their careers, and contribute higher-quality posts themselves.

Success Metrics

Content Relevance Score

User surveys on how relevant their feed content feels to their professional goals and interests.

Engagement Quality

Ratio of meaningful comments to surface-level reactions. Higher quality engagement indicates more valuable content.

Time Well Spent

User-reported satisfaction with time spent on LinkedIn. Are users learning and growing from their feed experience?

Content Diversity

Reduction in duplicate or template-based content. Increase in original, industry-specific insights.
