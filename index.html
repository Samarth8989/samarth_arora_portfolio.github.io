The Aha Moment

We had machines selling ₹3K/month while others hit ₹25K—same building, different floors. Traditional snacks offered 12-15% margins with constant price pressure from distributors.

During routine site visits, I started noticing patterns: machines near meeting areas sold more afternoon snacks, but office workers kept asking 'do you have protein bars?' We were stocking what vendors pushed, not what office workers actually wanted.

How I Diagnosed This

1

Office Behavior Pattern Analysis

I spent weeks observing different office locations to understand when, where, and what employees actually wanted to buy.

What I Discovered
  • Machines near meeting areas sold 3x more during afternoon hours
  • Lobby machines had morning health-conscious purchases
  • Late-shift workers bought energy drinks, not chips
  • Employees consistently asked for protein bars and nuts
  • Lunch areas needed quick meal replacements, not snacks
15 Office Locations
3 Weeks Observation
2

Margin Analysis & Vendor Research

I analyzed our margins across different product categories and discovered healthy snacks had much higher profit potential.

Key Findings
  • Traditional snacks: 12-15% margins with price pressure
  • Healthy options: 35% margins but limited availability
  • Premium protein bars: 40% margins, high demand
  • Nuts and dried fruits: 30% margins, consistent sales
  • Energy drinks: 25% margins, location-dependent demand
2.3x Better Margins
12 New Vendors
3

Wellness Demand Gap Identification

I surveyed office workers and facility managers to understand the disconnect between what we stocked and what they actually wanted.

Survey Results
  • 78% wanted healthier snacking options at work
  • 65% willing to pay premium for quality healthy snacks
  • Facility managers interested in promoting employee wellness
  • Current mix was 80% chips and cookies, 20% everything else
  • Ideal mix requested: 40% healthy, 35% traditional, 25% beverages
200+ Responses
8 Companies Surveyed

What I Built

1

Strategic Machine Repositioning

I moved underperforming machines to better locations within offices and negotiated with facility managers for prime wellness spots.

How I Did It
  • Mapped foot traffic patterns in each office building
  • Negotiated with facility managers for cafeteria and break room spots
  • Relocated 45 machines from lobbies to high-traffic areas
  • Created "wellness corners" near gyms and meditation rooms
  • Established partnerships with HR teams for health initiatives
45 Machines Relocated
2.1x Location Performance
2

Wellness-Focused Inventory Strategy

I piloted a new inventory mix focused on healthy options in 120 select machines positioned as "office wellness corners."

Pilot Program Details
  • Sourced protein bars, nuts, and organic snacks from 8 new vendors
  • Created 27% healthy options mix during peak hours
  • Added energy items specifically for late-shift workers
  • Branded select machines as "Wellness Stations"
  • Tracked sales data weekly to optimize product mix
120 Pilot Machines
8 Wellness Vendors
3

Performance Monitoring System

I created a simple tracking system to monitor which healthy products worked where, allowing rapid optimization across the network.

Tracking System
  • Weekly sales reports comparing traditional vs healthy products
  • Location-specific performance dashboards
  • Customer feedback collection through QR codes
  • Vendor performance scorecards for margin optimization
  • Monthly reviews with facility managers on employee satisfaction
Weekly Data Reviews
